What is BMR?

Basal Metabolic Rate is the minimum energy (kcal) your body needs to maintain vital functions — breathing, circulation, cell repair — while at complete rest. For most men, BMR accounts for 60–75% of total daily burn.

BMRmale = 10×kg + 6.25×cm − 5×age + 5
Mifflin-St Jeor (1990)

BMR vs RMR vs TDEE

  • BMR — strict lab conditions, fully rested
  • RMR — resting metabolic rate, ~10% higher
  • TDEE — BMR × activity factor + TEF
  • Most calculators report BMR; daily eating targets use TDEE

Lean-mass formulas

When body composition is known, lean-mass equations often outperform height/weight estimates for trained men.

Katch-McArdle: 370 + 21.6 × Lean (kg)
Cunningham: 500 + 22 × Lean (kg)

FAQ

Which BMR formula is most accurate for men?

Mifflin-St Jeor (1990) is the most widely validated for general populations — typically within ±10% of measured BMR. For lean or muscular men, Katch-McArdle using body fat % is often closer. Harris-Benedict tends to overestimate slightly in modern cohorts.

Should I eat at my BMR to lose weight?

No — eating at BMR ignores activity and digestion. Most men need a deficit from TDEE, not BMR. Never eat below BMR for extended periods; 1,500 kcal/day is a common safety floor. Consult a physician before aggressive cuts.

How is BMR measured in a lab?

Gold standard is indirect calorimetry — measuring oxygen consumption and CO₂ production after overnight fast, 8+ hours sleep, no exercise for 24 hours, and lying still in a thermoneutral room (20–25°C).

What lowers or raises BMR?

Muscle mass, thyroid function, genetics, and body size raise BMR. Age, prolonged dieting, insufficient sleep, and low protein intake can lower it. Chronic stress and illness also affect resting burn.

BMR vs TDEE — which do I use for meal planning?

Use TDEE (BMR × activity level) for daily calorie targets. BMR tells you your resting floor and helps you understand how much activity adds. Add ~10% for thermic effect of food when planning meals.

When should I recalculate BMR?

Recalculate after gaining or losing 10–15 lbs, significant body composition change, turning 40+, or after 12+ weeks of consistent dieting when weight loss stalls despite adherence.
